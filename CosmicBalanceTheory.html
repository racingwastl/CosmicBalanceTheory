Foundation

Two Axioms
& a Bridge

The CBT rests on two foundational axioms connected by the conservation of energy. From this minimal foundation, three major consequences follow — each addressing one of cosmology's greatest open problems.

Axiom I
Mass Is Energy

There are no fundamentally different kinds of matter. There is only energy in different states. The 5 / 27 / 68 partition of the standard model is a classification by our measurement capability — not by physics.

Axiom II
The Sum Is Zero

The cosmos is a zero-sum game. Positive energy (mass, radiation) is exactly compensated by negative energy (gravity). The zero-sum principle extends to all conserved quantum numbers.

Bridge — Conservation of Energy
+1 − 1 = 0

Because there is only one currency — energy — a total sum can be formed. In a closed system, that sum is conserved. CBT postulates: this sum is exactly zero.

Three Consequences

What the Axioms
Demand

Three long-standing problems of cosmology are resolved — not by adding new particles or fields, but by reinterpreting what we already observe.

1
Dark Matter Is Collapsed Energy

Black holes are gravitationally blind to their content (no-hair theorem). CBT identifies dark matter as primordial black holes (PBHs) — collapsed energy from the early universe in the mass range 10¹⁴ to 10¹⁹ kg (asteroid to lunar mass). This window remains largely open as of 2026, and PBHs can account for all of dark matter. After over 30 years of null results in direct WIMP searches, non-particle DM candidates gain significant plausibility.

Collapsed Energy · PBHs · No New Particles
2
Antimatter Is Hidden, Not Gone

The apparent baryon asymmetry (η ≈ 6×10⁻¹⁰) is not a fundamental puzzle. Before PBH formation, the net baryon number was zero. Regions with a slight antimatter excess were statistically correlated with overdensities and preferentially collapsed into PBHs. After collapse, baryon number is hidden — but the corresponding positive baryon number remains as free matter. The ALPHA-g experiment (CERN, 2023) confirms antimatter falls like matter. What distinguishes is the state: collapsed or free.

B = 0 · Sakharov Conditions Circumvented
3
Dark Energy Is Geometry

Dark energy is not a third substance alongside matter and radiation. It is the geometric consequence of the universe being flat (Ω_total = 1, confirmed by Planck) — because it is a zero-sum game. The 68% attributed to "dark energy" is the difference that brings the total sum to zero. This reinterpretation also addresses the coincidence problem and the flatness problem: flatness is not a dynamical result but a logical requirement of the zero-sum axiom.

Geometric Zero Sum · Ω = 1 · No New Substance
